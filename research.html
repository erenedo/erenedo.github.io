The Price of Globalization: Job Reallocation and Risk Aversion in an Open Economy

with Nezih Guner, Alessandro Ruggieri, and, James Tybout.

We develop a dynamic open economy model to study how globalization affects workers' wealth and welfare, focusing on income risk and job security in Colombia. Results show that while lowering trade barriers yields an average welfare gain of over 2%, low-wealth workers face welfare losses around 2%, whereas wealthier workers experience significant welfare improvements.

Taxes and Transfers with Nonlinear Wage Dynamics

with Nezih Guner, and, Emre Enes Yavuz.

Using U.S. panel data, we estimate a nonlinear, non-normal wage shock process and embed it into a life-cycle model with income risk and social insurance. Compared to standard models, the nonlinear shock process implies greater income mobility and lower persistence, making social insurance less valuable for poor households but more valuable for richer ones.

Boom and Bust in House Prices and Consumption: The Role of Macroprudential Policies

This paper builds and estimates an equilibrium, heterogeneous-agents, incomplete-markets macro-housing model of the Spanish economy to assess the impact of loan-to-value (LTV) limits on the housing boom and bust cycle between 1998 and 2014. Counterfactual simulations show that tighter LTV constraints would have dampened both house price volatility and the sharp consumption collapse during the bust. Moreover, the analysis finds that these alternative macroprudential regimes would have generated average welfare gains.
